Autobiography

I am an only child, and growing up, I was taught to work hard for everything I want to achieve. My parents always reminded me that success doesn’t come easy, so I learned to be disciplined and determined. I graduated as the valedictorian in elementary and with the highest honors from De La Salle University-Dasmariñas in senior high school. Ever since I was young, I’ve always been competitive, especially when it comes to academics. However, I try not to pressure myself too much because I know that burnout can be overwhelming.

I have a constant urge to prove myself, not just to others but also to myself. I always set high expectations, which pushes me to improve and do better. One of the things I enjoy is solving the Rubik’s Cube, which helps me develop my problem-solving skills. I also like engaging in challenges that require logic and strategy. I believe that every problem has a solution, and I like the process of figuring things out step by step.

Aside from academics, I also value self-discipline and personal growth. I try to be organized in everything I do because it helps me manage my time better. While I can be serious and focused, I also enjoy spending time with my friends and taking breaks when needed. I know that success is not just about achievements but also about finding balance in life. In the future, I want to continue pushing myself to improve while also learning to enjoy the journey. My goal is to keep growing, both academically and personally, while staying true to who I am.
